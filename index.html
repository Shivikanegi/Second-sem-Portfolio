UX Case Study · Born in Rishikesh

Safar

Group Trip Coordinator

How It Started

During a two-day sketching trip to Rishikesh, coordination broke down completely — dead batteries, a scattered group, and total confusion over who paid for what during meals and transport. Hours were lost to logistics and awkward math instead of sketching.

On the bus ride home, I realized we needed a unified tool to coordinate routes, stay connected, and split bills seamlessly on the go. That became Safar — tested on real friends, refined through real trips.

What It Does
📍
Real-Time Location

See everyone without constantly texting

🛜
Offline Mode

Works without data, syncs when reconnected

📋
Shared Itinerary

One place for plans — no endless group chats

🔋
Battery-Friendly

Dark mode by default, built for 5% battery

💸
Expense Splitting

Track who paid what during the trip. Safar calculates each person's share automatically — no more mental math, awkward reminders, or "I'll pay you back" moments that never happen.
